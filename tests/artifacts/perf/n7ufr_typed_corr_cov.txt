br-frankenpandas-n7ufr — Typed Float64 fast path for Series.corr/cov + DataFrame.corrwith
=========================================================================================

Fifth in the eydcr Scalar-bound-elementwise-ops family (diff 30x, pct_change 38x,
combine_first 5.6x, ffill/bfill 18-19x). Series.corr/cov accumulated the paired
sums over `aligned_numeric_pairs`, which align(Inner)-reindexes both columns,
materializes Vec<Scalar>, and runs per-element to_f64 — even for typed Float64.
DataFrame.corrwith calls Series::corr per shared column.

LEVER (one): typed_corr_sums helper accumulates (sum_x, sum_y, sum_xy, sum_x2,
sum_y2, count) directly from the two f64 slices + validity masks, gated on an
IDENTICAL UNIQUE index. align_inner for identical unique indexes yields positions
0..n on both sides (each label maps to its own row), so the kept pairs are exactly
(x[i], y[i]) for every i where both are valid, in ascending i order. Wired into
cov_components (used by corr/corrwith) and cov_pair_sums (used by cov).

ISOMORPHISM (f64-sum order matters — proven identical):
  - align_inner walks left.labels in order, keeping labels present in right; for
    identical unique indexes that is positions 0..n on both sides, in ascending
    order. So aligned_numeric_pairs yields (self[i], other[i]) for i=0..n with
    missing pairs dropped — exactly the order/values typed_corr_sums folds.
  - A pair is kept iff both sides are non-missing-non-NaN. A set validity bit
    already implies a non-NaN present f64 (Float64 NaN is missing), matching
    pairwise_numeric_value's `to_f64().ok().filter(!is_nan)`.
  - The 5 sums are accumulated with the identical separate `+=` left-fold and the
    identical f64 products (x*y, x*x, y*y); the downstream cov/var/corr arithmetic
    in cov_components / cov_with_options is unchanged.
  - Gated to identical+unique index and Float64 backings; differing/duplicate
    indexes or any other dtype keep the exact Scalar path.
