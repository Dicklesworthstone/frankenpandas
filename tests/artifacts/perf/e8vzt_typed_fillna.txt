br-frankenpandas-e8vzt — Typed Float64 fast path for Column.fillna
==================================================================

eydcr family. Column::fillna (fp-columnar; Series/DataFrame.fillna delegate to
it per column) materialized Vec<Scalar>, cloned per element, and rebuilt via
Self::new revalidation even for typed Float64.

LEVER (one): when the column is Float64 and the (dtype-cast) fill value is a
finite f64, read raw f64 + validity via Column::as_f64_slice_with_validity and
fill every missing slot with the fill into an all-valid typed buffer, emitting
via Column::from_f64_values.

BENCH (gap_hunt, release-perf, n=200000 x 8 cols, ms/iter, min-of-5; loaded):
  DataFrame.fillna(0.0):  47    ->  3.8  = 12.4x

SCORE: 12.4x >> 2.0 PASS.

ISOMORPHISM (per-element select; no arithmetic):
  - Present slot (validity set AND datum not NaN — a valid-bit NaN is treated as
    missing by Scalar::is_missing, matching the general `if v.is_missing()`
    check): keeps its f64, a present Float64.
  - Missing slot: takes the finite fill `fv`, a present Float64 — exactly the
    general path's `cast_fill.clone()`.
  - Every result is finite, so from_f64_values yields an all-valid column, exactly
    what Self::new derives from the all-Float64-finite scalar vector.
  - Gated to Float64 dtype + a finite Float64 fill (a NaN/non-Float64 fill keeps
    the Scalar path, where fill-with-missing semantics still apply). The Null-dtype
    branch above is untouched.
  - Golden sha256 (gap_hunt golden: the full elementwise family + fillna of a
    nullable frame with two finite constants and an all-valid no-op, n=5000 x4)
